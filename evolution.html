Pre-20th Century Mechanical Calculators

Abacus (c. 3000 BCE) :

- One of the earliest computing devices used for arithmetic calculations.
- Consists of beads that can be moved along rods to represent numbers.

19th Century: Conceptual Foundations

Difference Engine (1822) :

- Designed by Charles Babbage, an early mechanical computer intended to compute polynomial functions.
- The project was never completed, but it laid the groundwork for future computers.

Early 20th Century: Electromechanical and Early Electronic Computers

Tabulating Machine (1890) :

- Created by Herman Hollerith for the U.S. Census Bureau.
- Used punched cards to store and process data, significantly speeding up the census process.

Mid-20th Century: The Advent of Transistors and Integrated Circuits

Transistor (1947) :

- Invented by John Bardeen, Walter Brattain, and William Shockley at Bell Labs.
- Replaced bulky vacuum tubes, leading to smaller, more reliable, and energy-efficient computers.

Late 20th Century: Microprocessors and Personal Computers

Intel 4004 (1971) :

- The first microprocessor, developed by Intel.
- Integrated all the functions of a computer's central processing unit (CPU) on a single chip.

Modern Era: Advances in Computing Technology

Graphical User Interface (GUI) :

- Popularized by Apple Macintosh (1984) and Microsoft Windows (1985).
- Made computers more user-friendly and accessible to the general public.

Modern Era: Advances in Computing Technology

Internet and World Wide Web (1990s) :

- Revolutionized how computers are used, enabling global connectivity and information sharing.
